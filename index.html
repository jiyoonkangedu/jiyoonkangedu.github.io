About

Researcher, teacher, and drama educator.

I am a Ph.D. student in Teaching & Learning at The Ohio State University. My work is shaped by my experience as an elementary school teacher in South Korea and my ongoing interest in drama-based pedagogy, critical literacy, and culturally responsive teaching.

My current research explores how drama and multimodal literacy practices can support immigrant and heritage language learners’ identity, belonging, agency, and critical engagement with texts.

Research

Research Interests

Critical Literacy

I examine how young learners read, question, and redesign texts in relation to identity, power, language, and culture.

Drama-Based Pedagogy

I explore how drama, embodiment, role, and improvisation can create spaces for meaning-making and critical inquiry.

Korean Heritage Education

I am interested in Korean heritage classrooms as community-based spaces for language, culture, identity, and belonging.

Teacher Education

I study how teachers learn to design equitable, creative, and responsive curriculum across linguistic and cultural contexts.

Teaching

Teaching & Practice

Before beginning my doctoral studies, I taught in public elementary schools in South Korea. My teaching experiences include elementary literacy, arts integration, multicultural education, and drama-based classroom practice.

  • Elementary teacher, South Korea
  • Korean-American community school teaching
  • Drama-based literacy and arts-integrated instruction
  • Interest in immigrant students’ belonging, agency, and recognition
